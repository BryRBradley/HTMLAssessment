A little about Disc Golf

Disc Golf is a sport similar to traditional golf but played with flying discs (often called frisbees) instead of clubs and balls. The objective of disc golf is to complete each hole in the fewest throws possible, just like in regular golf.

Typically played on a course with 9 or 18 holes, players start from a designated tee area and aim to throw their disc towards a target, usually a metal basket with hanging chains that catch the disc. The holes vary in length and difficulty, with obstacles like trees, bushes, and terrain adding to the challenge. Players select different types of discs for various shots, including drivers for long-distance throws, mid-range discs for controlled approaches, and putters for short, accurate shots near the target. The sport requires a combination of technique, strategy, and mental focus.

Disc Golf Disc golf is enjoyed by people of all ages and skill levels, and it can be played casually with friends or competitively in organized tournaments. It promotes outdoor recreation, physical activity, and community engagement, with courses located in parks, forests, and other scenic areas around the world.
